They wanted to emphasize Harris' potential historic moment being elected as the first asian-american and black woman vice president, and counter any racist or sexist attacks that came her way duing the election season.

The Solution: I worked with one of the art directors at SKDK to develop a few design directions within their existing but underdeveloped brand. Upon picking a direction after consulting with the broader Vote For Her team, we closely followed the news and responded to any attacks on the Biden Harris ticket as well as elevate their general plan.

With the help of the public affairs Vote For Her team, we closely followed the news to respond to current events. We posted content on both twitter and facebook, resizing any design for both platforms.

During the vice presidential debate between Mike Pence and Kamala Harris, one of the art directors and I were in a war room pulling clips from the live footage. The following morning, we decided to compile a montage of footage of Pence interrupting Harris and the moderator. I worked to pull the clips and compile the video in only 3 hours, so we could be the first to drop the video on twitter. The Problem: In my Data Visualization and Infographic class at the Rhode Island School of Design, my professor asked us to create an infographic that showed three different trips all starting from the Biltmore Hotel in Providence, RI. One trip needed to end at the design center, one at our house, and one abroad. Each trip needed to show at least 15 different events as well as how long the trip took.

The Solution: Instead of showing the length of time each trip took, I decided to show carbon footprint of each trip and how my methods of transportation contributed to the overall footprint. All the art and icons were created by hand in Adobe Illustrator.

The infographic is designed to keep your attention and reveal more and more layers of information the closer you look. Upon first glance you may only notice the title, Earth illustration, or carbon emission graph, but then you may notice the carbon footprint definition or even the conversation I had with the TSA worker at the JFK airport.

The Problem: In my Data Visualization and Infographic class at the Rhode Island School of Design, my professor asked us to create an infographic that compiled ten different infographics for one country.

The Solution: I created this accordion book of Japan by designing the art in adobe illustrator and laying it out in Adobe InDesign. The book is cut so that the left folio is two inches shorter than the right folio. The top two inches of the right folio are cut so that each layer represents part of Japan's culture-a tsunami wave, buddhist temples, bonsai trees, mount fuji, and the japanese flag.

The Problem: In my internship at Project Chimps, the country's newest chimpanzee refuge located in Morganton Georgia, I was tasked with creating a sign that would inform visitors of the ecological significance of apiary's and their role in their facility.

The Solution: I researched apiary's and talked to the workers at Project Chimps and created a large (18x24in) sign of what I felt was important information to share with the public. I used secondary colors in Project Chimp's brand so as to stay within their brand but carve out a sub-brand that would suit the apiary.

The Problem: In my Book Design class at the Rhode Island School of Design, my professor asked us to redesign a book of our choosing.

The Solution: I chose Field Notes from a Catastrophe by Elizabeth Kolbert. The book cover was designed in Adobe Photoshop and the book was designed in Adobe InDesign.

The cover is inspired by a Rite in the Rain Journal, which is the most popular note-taking book for ecologists due to its weatherproof qualities. Rite in the Rain's have an iconic yellow cover and while I thought it would be recognized by those likely to read this book, I also thought that the yellow inspired caution which seemed appropriate for a book about climate change.

Hi there! I am a graphic designer, web designer, and artist living in Bend Oregon.

Painting and drawing were my main introductions into the creative world, which led to my fascination and pursuit of graphic and web design. Above all else, I care about using design to tell meaningful stories.

Graphic Design

Upon graduating from Bowdoin College in 2019, I found myself at the Rhode Island School of Design where I first found my infatuation with using design as both an artform and form of communication.

The following fall I interned at Project Chimps as a graphic design and communications intern. I then joined SKDK, voted the top public affairs agency by the Holmes report in 2019, as a graphic design intern and was promoted that summer to creative consultant so as to help the creative department with the workload during election season.

To me, design is about storytelling.

Web Design

At RISD, I took a class in Web Design and since have been teaching myself Javascript. I love the challenge of problem solving and achieving a design with code.

I am also taking classes in UI/UX to learn the fundamentals of user-oriented design as well as applications such as Figma and Adobe XD.

Studio Art

My foundation is grounded in my painting, drawing, and printmaking practice. Using my hands on paper to create art helps my graphic design practice and vice versa.

I feel most in my roots in the messiness of oil paints and charcoal as well as testing ink through the printing press. Check out my work here!
