The problem: In my Book Design class at the Rhode Island School of Design, my professor asked us to create a book with a complex hierarchy such as a cookbook or travel book.

The solution: With more interest in dogs than cooking, I chose to create a dog breed book. All the dog illustrations were done by hand in Adobe Fresco.

This book is entirely crafted from my imagination, so luckily, I was able to design the content to the layout I had in mind. I had just bought an Apple Pencil and wanted to experiment with drawing on my iPad to create the dog imagery. Since the illustrations ended up being more on the abstract side of each spread, I emphasized the grid structure on the side with the information to create a juxtaposition. I ended up using a slab-serif font, Mortise, for the headlines along with a sans-serif font, Gentona, for the body copy. While each page that you see has a different background and headline color, I imagine the rest of the pages to alternate between those four.

One issue I am still struggling with is that I feel the book needs a photo identification for each species. If this is to be a true breed book, I imagine a supplementary photo ID would be necessary. I think that it could potentially go inline on the text side of the page similar to the "Did You Know?" image. Nevertheless, I am quite pleased with the final outcome. I especially enjoyed the challenge of creating the illustrations and then creating a clean layout to balance the abstract images.
