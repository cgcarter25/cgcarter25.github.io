The problem: In my Data Visualization and Infographic class at the Rhode Island School of Design, my professor asked us to create an infographic that compiled ten different infographics for one country.

The solution: I created this accordion book of Japan by designing the art in adobe illustrator and laying it out in Adobe InDesign. The book is cut so that the left folio is two inches shorter than the right folio. The top two inches of the right folio are cut so that each layer represents part of Japan's culture-a tsunami wave, buddhist temples, bonsai trees, mount fuji, and the japanese flag.

This book is entirely crafted from my imagination, so in some ways I was able to design the content to the layout I had in mind. I had just bought an apple pencil and wanted to experiment with drawing on my iPad to create the illustrations. Becuase the illustrations ended up being more abstract, I wanted to emphasize my grid structure as a justaposition. I ended up using a slab-serif font, Mortise, for the headlines along with a sans-serif font, Gentona, for the body copy. While each page that you see has a different background and headline color, I imagine each further page to alternate between these four. The colors do not pertain to any specific group or breed of dog, but just add variation to the overall book.

One issue I am still struggling with is that I feel like the book needs a photo identification for each species. I am still working on how to incorporate the image. If this is to be a true breed book, I imagine a photo ID to be necessary. I think that it could potentially go inline on the text side of the page like with the "Did You Know?" image. Nevertheless, I am quite pleased with the final outcome. I especially enjoyed the challenge of creating the illustrations and then creating a clean layout to balance the abstract images.
