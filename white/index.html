CAROLINE CARTER

White and Clean

Book Design

In my Type and Image class at the Rhode Island School of Design, my professor asked me to spend some time thinking about “white” and then to design a project inspired by the concept of white. After researching different fields of the word, I decided to juxtapose two infamous texts based on the concept: The Holy Bible and Sir Isaac Newton's original "Opticks: Or a Treatise on the Reflexions, Refractions and Inflections, and Colors of Light." By interweaving mentions of white from these texts and editing them into a new story, along with inspired photography, I attempt to show these ideas in a new light. In doing so, I noticed religion occupied a motif of white representing purity in particular and delved more specifically into Catholicism. I also was drawn to the science of white light as a combination of all the colors in the rainbow. When broken up, white becomes a conglomeration of every color. It contains everything while showing nothing. In this way, I wanted to juxtapose science and religion as embodying very different meanings of white.

I used a projector to show selected quotes from my chosen texts on white clothes. For a couple pages, I also used a glass prism from an old chandelier to refract words and create interesting typographical images.

The final book is bound as an accordian to show a continuity between each page. In order to protect the whiteness and cleanliness of the final product I made a book cover.

Tools Used: Adobe Indesign & Photography
