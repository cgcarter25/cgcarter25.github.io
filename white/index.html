CAROLINE CARTER

White and Clean

Book Design

In my Type and Image class at the Rhode Island School of Design, my professor asked me to spend some time thinking about “white” and then to design a project inspired by the concept of white. After researching different fields of the word, I decided to juxtapose two infamous texts based on the concept: The Holy Bible and Sir Isaac Newton's original "Opticks: Or a Treatise on the Reflexions, Refractions and Inflections, and Colors of Light." By interweaving mentions of white from these texts and editing them into a new story, along with inspired photography, I attempt to show these ideas in a new light. When reading mentions of "white" in these texts, I noticed religion occupied symbol of purity and holiness. I was fascinated by the stark difference in Newton's descriptions of white light as a combination of all the colors in the rainbow. When broken up, white becomes a conglomeration of every color. It contains everything while showing nothing.

I decided to interweave mentions of white from these two texts and edit them into a new story, attempting to show these ideas in a new light. I used a projector to show selected quotes from my chosen texts on white clothes. I also used a glass prism, not unlike one Newton may have used himself, from an old chandelier to refract words and create interesting typographical images in a couple pages.

The final book is bound as an accordian to show a continuity between each page.

Tools Used: Adobe Indesign & Photography
