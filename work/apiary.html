The problem: In my internship at Project Chimps, I was asked to create a sign that would inform visitors about the facilityâ€™s apiary and the general ecological significance of honeybees.

The solution: I researched apiaries and talked to the workers at Project Chimps to create a large (18x24in) sign that would be displayed outside of the apiary on the grounds.

One of the trickier parts of this project was synthesizing all the information I gathered from my research to create a cohesive and educational graphic. I was told that the sign would be printed large-scale and displayed in front of the apiary, so that when visitors toured the facility, they could view the infographic. Given most of our visitors are on the older side or are children, I needed to ensure that the copy was appropriately sized and a visual was matched with each sub-infographic.

I especially enjoyed creating all the icons by hand in Adobe Illustrator. I typically prefer to create my own icons as opposed to downloading from free libraries because it is easier to maintain consistency in line-weight and overall look. This is especially important in a design such as an infographic because of the large volume of information in one space. I used colors from the secondary palette of the Project Chimps brand to maintain brand identity, but also used colors that invoke honeybees. The headlines all have their own hierarchy, as denoted with color and font. The only graphic that breaks this trend is the timeline at the bottom, but I figured that using white as a backdrop for that section broke up the information in a graphically interesting way.
