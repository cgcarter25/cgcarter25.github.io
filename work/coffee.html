The problem: I'm a coffee shop enthusiast, but I realized that I have no idea what the difference between a cappucino and a latt√© are.

The solution: I created an interactive web infographic to help understand the differences between various coffee and espresso drinks. I also wanted to work on my GSAP animiation skills.

Take a look inside here!

While there are many poster-type coffee infographics out there, I found them all to be a bit cluttered and tricky to notice the real differences between the drinks. Apart from wanting to work on my interactive web skills, I felt like interactivity would be key to keep a user's or reader's attention. This is why I added not only the animation in the glass cup but also the text below between each drink. Light animation is key in keeping a user interested in moving through the carousel of drinks. This infographic is also responsive and resizes for a tablet and phone width.

I experimented with several layouts and backgrounds, but ultimately landed on this more minimalist, bubblegum-pop vibe. Since coffee is so dark, a white background worked better for this design because it kept the attention focused on the cup and allowed for the supplemental text to be easier to read. The typefaces are inspired by what I would expect to see on a blackboard coffeeshop menu with big blocky letters as a title and handwritten coffe drink items below. I ended up adding some green accents to the piece because I felt like it balanced the browns of the drinks and gave it a lighter, more fun feel.
