Notes
Currently, the Labrador Retreiver reigns as the most popular dog breed and has done so since 1991. There are, however, some new breeds making their way into the top 10 like the Pembroke Welsh Corgi, which made the 10th spot in 2019. Dogs are the most popular pet for Americans, as 38.4% of households own at least one dog. In fact, the average number of dogs owned per household is 1.6, meaning most households that own a dog own more than one. It should be noted, however, that dog registration rates have decreased, suggesting that while Americans are still adopting dogs, fewer are registering their dogs.
Some studies have shown that owning a dog can help you maintain a more positive, optimistic persespective, essentially making you happier. Their unconditional love is shown to improve moods, decrease stress, and increase oxytocin. They also do so by "getting you off the couch" as they require exercise. This is universal across all breeds, and while there are breeds like the labrador retreiver and german shepherd which have been at the top of the ranks for a while, really any dog can give you the companionship and love that most seek out when buying a dog.
