The problem: As a creative consultant at SKDK, I worked with several organizations and campaigns at the presidential, congressional, state, and local level to design both positive and negative direct mail.

The solution: I designed, edited, prepressed, and proofed over 80 pieces for candidates running at the congressional, state, and local levels. I stayed within each candidate's existing brand, building it out for the candidates with less developed brands.

Working at SKDK during the 2020 election cycle certainly taught me some valuable time-management skills. At the peak of my workload during the fall, I was juggling over 30 different mail pieces on any given day. These ranged from pre-pressing pieces for print to crafting the design concepts from copy that was provided to me. Perhaps my favorite part of this entire project was that many of our client candidate’s brands were underdeveloped. This allowed for me to build out their brand from typically only a simple website. Due to copywriting rules, we often would need to create a new logo for the candidate that was still in their brand, but distinct enough from their existing logo. With only about 30 minutes to do so, this was always a fun challenge and drill to hone my logo-designing skills.

My workflow for these mail pieces was typically to draft a design from a sheet of copy that was given to me. I would then go back and forth a few times with my supervisor and the copywriter. These revisions provided me ample opportunities to discuss my work and choices and better understand how the design would be interpreted by the audiences for these pieces.
