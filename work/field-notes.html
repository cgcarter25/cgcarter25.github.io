The problem: In my Book Design class at the Rhode Island School of Design, my professor asked us to redesign a book of our choosing.

The solution: The assignment asks for a completely text-based book, so I chose one of my favorites: Field Notes from a Catastrophe by Elizabeth Kolbert.

This is the first book that I designed, and it follows a traditional novel layout. I especially enjoyed the challenge of creating a clean layout for all the different information on the back. I chose a narrow sans-serif for the headers to create a clean modern look. I went for a sans-serif for the copy to enhance readability. I also focused on line-height just as much as type-size as this can be even more important in readability. While the assignment did not require me to design a cover, after designing the interior, I felt it needed the covers to be complete.

The cover is inspired by a Rite in the Rain Journal, which is the most popular note-taking book for ecologists due to its weatherproof qualities. The audience for this book is geared towards environmentally minded people, so it is possible that they have encountered the infamous field notebook. Rite in the Rains have an iconic yellow cover, and while my motivation for using the color was due to its recognition by those likely to read this book, I also thought that the yellow inspired caution. This seemed appropriate for a book about climate change. I built off the notebook theme by creating a grid-line design for the endpapers and a torn paper effect on the front.
