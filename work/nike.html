The problem: Nikeâ€™s research team approached me asking for infographics that they could use to emphasize mental imagery training that they could send to some of their top athletes.

The solution: After some conversations about the project, we ultimately decided on an animated video with minimal text as well as a supplemental static one-pager.

I really enjoyed working with the team on this, mostly because the project was so open-ended. I started with reviewing the literature they provided me on the importance of mental training and preparing oneself for training and races before they actually happen. I then pared down the language into about 10 steps and created a couple versions of the static one-pager using different color palettes and fonts. Once we landed on a general style, I created a storyboard of the animation, and once that was approved went to the animation process itself. I wanted to try experimenting with sound, given how little text there would be on each slide. I ultimately used a free sound library and found relatable sounds like breathing and turning on a car engine to help bring clarity to the animation itself.

I really enjoyed making the animation especially and working in After Effects. We iterated through a few versions with different amounts of text, testing them out on the other members of the Nike research team who had little knowledge about this mental imagery technique. Theoretically, an athlete watching this video has been queued into the importance of mental imagery training but does not know how exactly to practice it.
