The problem: Michigan Senator Gary Peters’ campaign approached SKDK, asking for a social and digital ad strategy to enhance their campaign and share their message.

The solution: As a creative consultant at SKDK, I was the lead designer for over 100 social graphics and digital ad banners for their campaign.

Peters was facing a tough re-election against his opponent John James. This meant that all throughout September, October, and the beginning of November the campaign wanted us to put out targeted ads with key messaging. My workflow with most of these was for a copywriter to give me the language that they were thinking about using in the ad, and for me to create the design. Peters already had somewhat of a brand established, so I typically would try to create two or three options for the team to look at.

I was given access to a database with hundreds of photos, and it was always my responsibility to find the best imagery for each ad. Given the importance of name/face recognition with political ads, we wanted to use strong imagery of Senator Peters usually in the community or in nature, to show his commitment to the state and its people. We also were asked to create a few negative targeted ads of John James. These were especially tricky because I needed to carve out a subsection of Peters’ brand that would work for an attack ad. I ended up using an entirely new color palette grounded in reds and blacks but kept the same typefaces. This I felt created the distinction necessary from the existing positive Peters ads without straying too far from the original brand.
