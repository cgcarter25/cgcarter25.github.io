RISD

The problem: In my Identity for Design Branding class at the Rhode Island School of Design, we were tasked with redesigning the RISD Museum's brand.

The solution: Over the course of two weeks, I redesigned their logo, stationary set, and website in a set of brand guidelines. As an additional project, I also created a logo animation.

The first thing I did when assigned this project was walk across the street and spend a few hours in the museum itself. Whenever I am given the opportunity to experience a place or product while in a branding project, I always take it. I feel like it’s the best way to understand what I am designing. While walking through the museum, I made a list of keywords that I felt described the space and experience. When I got back to the classroom, I edited, combined, and narrowed down the keywords to about 10 that I felt best encapsulated the museum. I then sat down at my computer and designed as many logos as I could think of. I tried to not get bogged down in the intricacies of the logos at the first stage, and just get as many directions as possible. After multiple rounds of feedback, I landed on one logo and felt I could continue to the brand book.

The logo is the heart of any brand, so creating the rest of the brand materials and logo animation came with more ease. I ended up adding a slogan to their brand “Find Connection,” though opted not to include it in their logo for fear of too much clutter. The logo animation was my first experience in Adobe After Effects, so though it may not seem like much of a feat, I am quite proud of the end result.
