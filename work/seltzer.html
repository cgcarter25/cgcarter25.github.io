Rebrand Design

The problem: I wanted to practice my branding skills and take on a fun personal project.

The solution: I decided to refresh the logos and can designs for three popular seltzer companies, whose logos I felt could use a refresh. While not a seltzer-drinker myself, I like to admire fun can designs and logos.

This project came to mind after watching my roommates go through different seltzer brands (depending on whatever was on sale) and feeling like some of the brands really needed a facelift. I started with La Croix, and while I already quite liked La Croix’s can design, I wanted to elevate its logo and overall look. I kept in the same hand-written feel of their logo and created a more feminine, cursive-looking logo. I then tackled Polar and Schweppes. I didn’t like these logos as much and wanted to give them a more modern and minimal feel. In doing some research, I noticed that both of these companies have been around for over 150 years. I built off the historic element of these brands in added a note of when they were founded, and also keeping somewhat of a retro feel for their brands.

Designing the cans was quite fun because I had never designed cans before. I worked off a free template and took most of the elements that were already on the can. I had to find new typefaces for each brand, because the logos ended up being more abstract and graphic, so I had manipulated the typefaces I used. I ended up creating a quick one-pager of brand guidelines for each brand because I needed more structure than a logo to create the cans.
