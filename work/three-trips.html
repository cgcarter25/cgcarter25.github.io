The problem: In my Data Visualization and Infographic Design class at the Rhode Island School of Design, my professor asked us to create an infographic that showed three trips all starting from the Providence Hotel near campus.

The solution: I created a poster infographic that showed one trip that stayed on campus, one trip that brought me home to Massachusetts, and one trip that brought me out of the country.

This is one of my all-time favorite designs and assignments I have been ever given, because there were so many layers and elements to consider. Our professor requested that we have at least 10 different elements or experiences along the way of each trip and that we calculate the time each trip took. I wanted to add another layer to the project, so after talking to my professor, I decided to calculate carbon emissions instead of time for each trip. I also ended up adding far more than 10 different elements to the longer two of the three trips.

When I landed on the carbon emission theme for this poster, I knew I didnâ€™t want to create a predictable green and white poster. Instead, I opted for a dark palette with blue and yellow secondary colors. I felt that the teal blue was indicative of the Earth and also easier to see on a dark background, and that the yellow inspired caution and grabbed the viewer's attention. I created all the icons by hand in Adobe Illustrator. The layout was probably the trickiest part of this poster because I knew I wanted to include a graph that showed the different emissions of each transportation and an illustration of the Earth. I spent a long time on the type-size and placement of each aspect of the infographic to create a guided experience for the viewer of this poster.
